# Idea: Advisor role — strategic consigliere for backlog and priority guidance

**Captured:** 2026-03-21
**Status:** raw

---

## The Idea

A new interactive role — the Advisor (or Consigliere) — that helps the human decide what to work on next. Unlike the Herald (which surfaces signals) or the Taskmaster (which refines a specific task), the Advisor takes a strategic view across the whole project state:

- Reads all ideas and recommends which should be refined next
- Reviews deferred tasks and recommends which should be undeferred into the active pool
- Looks at the backlog shape (raw/proposed/ready distribution) and spots imbalances
- Understands the vision for the next version(s) and uses that context to guide recommendations
- Surfaces connections ("this idea relates to that deferred task")

The Advisor is strictly advisory — it recommends, it doesn't force. The human is always in charge. Think consigliere, not CEO.

---

## Why This Is Worth Doing

Right now the Herald surfaces what's happening, but nothing surfaces what *should* happen next. As the idea and task backlog grows, the human needs a thinking partner who can see the whole board and help prioritize. This role fills the gap between "here's the current state" (Herald) and "let's refine this task" (Taskmaster) — it answers the question in between: "which task/idea should I even be looking at?"

---

## Open Questions / Things to Explore

- How does this relate to the Herald? Herald surfaces signals (stalled tasks, cold ideas); Advisor makes strategic recommendations. Clear enough boundary, or too much overlap?
- What context does the Advisor need to load? Vision doc, milestone plans, all ideas, all tasks — that's a lot of context. Progressive loading?
- Should the Advisor have access to a "milestone" or "roadmap" concept that doesn't exist yet?
- Is this a session role (like Oracle/Taskmaster) or an always-present skill (like Herald)?
- Name: Advisor, Consigliere, Steward, Strategist?
